🧩 Your Variables Meaning
mean

Average alignment score in that year.

median

Middle alignment score in that year.

count_x

Number of papers published in that year.

std

Standard deviation = how spread out alignment scores were that year.

🔥 Interpretation of YOUR Heatmap
1️⃣ Mean vs Median = 0.97

Very strong positive correlation.

Meaning:

Years with higher average alignment also have higher median alignment.

👉 This means results are stable and not distorted by outliers.

Very good sign.

2️⃣ Mean vs Count = -0.27

Weak negative relationship.

Meaning:

Years with more published papers tend to have slightly lower thematic alignment.

Possible explanation:

When more papers are accepted, topic diversity increases.

Interesting discussion point.

3️⃣ Mean vs Std = -0.78

Strong negative relationship.

Meaning:

Years with higher average alignment have lower variability.

👉 In years where journal strongly focuses on core themes, papers are also more consistent.

This is actually a strong insight.

4️⃣ Count vs Std = 0.11

Almost no relationship.

Meaning:

Publishing more papers doesn’t strongly change score variability.

Use something like:

Correlation analysis revealed strong consistency between yearly mean and median alignment scores (r = 0.97), indicating robust central tendency.
